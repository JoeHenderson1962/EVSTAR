DISPLAY ISSUES

Touchscreen is not working

Step 1: Fix touchscreen problems
If your touchscreen isn't working, try these steps. Test after each step to see whether your touchscreen works.
  • Wipe off any dust or dirt on your touchscreen.
  • Reset your Chromebook hardware

CHROMEBOOK CRASHES OR FREEZES

Step 1: Chromebook crashes or freezes

  • Turn off your Chromebook, then turn it back on.
  • Close all your apps and browser windows.
  • If a specific tab in your browser causes your Chromebook to crash or freeze, hard refresh the page: Ctrl + Shift + r.
  • If you've installed any new apps or extensions recently, uninstall them.
  • Reset your Chromebook

CHROMEBOOK BATTERY WON’T CHARGE

Step 1: Check hardware connections
  • First, check that:
  • The charger or adapter cables are completely plugged in, both to your Chromebook and the wall.
  • The power outlet is working.
Step 2: If your Chromebook still won't charge:
  • Unplug your charger from the wall and your Chromebook.
  • Plug your charger back in to your Chromebook, then the wall.
  • Charge the device for at least 30 minutes.
  • If the battery still won't charge, reset your Chromebook

CHROMEBOOK KEYBOARD WON'T WORK

Step 1: Volume or Brightness keys not working
  • If the volume or brightness keys don't work, you might be at the upper or lower limit for that setting.
Step 2: Backward or Forward key issues
  • If the backward or forward buttons don't work, check if the same icons in a web browser window are grayed out. For example, if the back button on a web page is gray, the browser isn't aware of a page to move backward to.
Step 3: Other Keyboard issues
  • Turn off the Chromebook and then turn it back on.
  • Use the keys while you browse as a guest. If the keys work, delete the account that has the problem on your Chromebook and then add it again.
  • Complete a hard reset on your Chromebook hardware.

TOUCHPAD DOESN'T WORK

Step 1: Fix touchpad problems
  • If your touchpad stops working, try these steps:
    • Make sure there is no dust or dirt on the touchpad.
    • Press the Esc key several times.
    • Drumroll your fingers on the touchpad for ten seconds.
    • Turn your Chromebook off, then back on again.
    • Perform a hard reset.
    • If you have more than one account on your Chromebook, delete the account that has problems with the touchpad, then re-add the account.
